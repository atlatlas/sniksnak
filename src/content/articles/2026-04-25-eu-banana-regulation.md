## A Fruitful Debate

In what can only be described as the greatest triumph of European bureaucracy since the regulation on cucumber straightness, the European Parliament has spent the better part of a decade deciding exactly how curved a banana is allowed to be. The result: **Banana Regulation 2026/042**, which stipulates that no banana sold within the EU may exceed a curvature of **1.3 centimeters per 10 centimeters of length**.

> "This is a proud day for European standards," said MEP Hans-Werner Krummbanane during the final vote, which passed with an overwhelming majority after three separate recounts and one incident involving a misplaced lunch.

The regulation, which will cost an estimated **€47 million** to enforce, requires every bunch of bananas to be individually inspected by certified curvature assessors — a new profession that the EU has helpfully created alongside the regulation itself. Job creation, some might call it. Others might call it an elaborate scheme to employ the cousins of Brussels bureaucrats.

## The Science Behind the Curve

According to the 340-page impact assessment, the curvature limit was determined after a **comprehensive study** involving 12,000 bananas from 27 member states. Researchers measured each banana's bend using laser-guided calipers in a climate-controlled laboratory in Luxembourg, because apparently no other place in Europe was suitable for such delicate work.

The study concluded that bananas with a curvature exceeding 1.3cm/10cm are **" aesthetically displeasing"** and may cause "consumer confusion." How exactly a banana confuses a consumer remains unexplained, but the report does include a helpful diagram showing the difference between an acceptable and unacceptable banana, which looks remarkably like the difference between any two bananas you might find in a supermarket.

## International Reaction

Costa Rica, the EU's largest banana supplier, has formally protested the regulation, calling it **"arbitrary and unscientific."** In response, the EU Commission's Chief Banana Negotiator stated that Costa Rica was welcome to file a complaint through the proper channels, which involves submitting a 200-page form in triplicate to an office in Strasbourg that is only open on Tuesdays between 10:00 and 11:30.

Ecuador has taken a different approach, announcing it will simply **sell its more curved bananas to China**, a market that has thus far expressed no opinion on banana geometry. The EU's trade commissioner described this as "regrettable but predictable," adding that China "simply doesn't understand the importance of produce uniformity."

## What This Means for You

Starting in January 2027, any banana that fails the curvature test must be labeled as a **"non-conforming fruit product"** and sold at a 15% discount. Supermarkets will be required to display a small sign near the banana section explaining the curvature standards, though nobody expects anyone to read it.

The regulation also includes provisions for **emergency banana straightening**, a procedure involving gentle pressure applied over 48 hours in a temperature-controlled environment. Scientists have confirmed this works approximately 30% of the time. The other 70% of the time, you simply have a sad, slightly less curved banana.

In a rare moment of self-awareness, the Parliament also voted to investigate whether the time and money spent on banana regulation could have been better used for, say, literally anything else. That report is expected in 2031.
